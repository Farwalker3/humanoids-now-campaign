# Collaborator assets & permission checklist

**Do not publish a partner's logo or product photo, or describe them as a partner, without written
OK from that party.** Early conversations are not endorsements. This page currently uses neutral
*slots* + accurate, modest wording on purpose. When you have permission, drop the official file into
`assets/` and replace the matching `<div class="slot">…</div>` with `<img src="assets/…">`.

## Per collaborator

### University of Michigan — Neurobionics Lab (Open-Source Leg)
- What: Elliott Rouse's lab; the **Open-Source Leg**, an open, modular bionic-limb research platform.
- Page wording (modest, factual): "We've spoken with the lab's director about prosthetics." ✅ safe.
- Assets & press: https://neurobionics.robotics.umich.edu/ · Open-Source Leg: https://opensourceleg.org/
- Permission: University logos are trademark-protected; get explicit OK before displaying the block-M
  or lab logo. Referencing/linking the open project and its open license is generally fine.

### EZ-Robot (JD Humanoid kit) — CEO Dennis Kambeitz
- What: accessible modular humanoid kit (16 servos, camera, LED eyes, ARC software).
- Page wording: "We've spoken with CEO Dennis Kambeitz … exploring for prototyping/education." ✅ safe.
- Assets: https://www.ez-robot.com (product images: JD Humanoid). Ask Dennis for a press/media kit +
  written OK to show the logo and a JD Humanoid photo on the campaign.
- Product image page: https://www.ez-robot.com/store/p5/humanoid-robotics-kit.html

### Raccoon AI — CONFIRMED: https://raccoonai.tech (general-purpose collaborative AI agent)
- Logo added: `assets/raccoonai_logo.png` (official wordmark from raccoonai.tech/logo.png). Shown on the page.
- You have permission for their **model**; ⚠️ confirm that permission also covers **displaying their
  logo** publicly and describing the relationship as stated. If not, ask them for explicit OK (usually a quick yes).
- Framing on page: their agent model powers the robot's "coworker" tasks (notes/reminders/tools) — accurate.
